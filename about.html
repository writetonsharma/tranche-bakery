About TRANCHÉ

Small-batch baking shaped by slower fermentation, simpler ingredients, and real kitchen process.

TRANCHÉ focuses on breads, buns, and European-inspired bakes made in smaller batches with more hands-on baking and a shorter distance between the oven and the table.

Our Story

Everyday bread, made with more patience than packaged bread usually gets.

We focus on loaves and rolls that work for real daily eating: toast, sandwiches, breakfast plates, table bread, and a growing set of European-inspired bakes. That means balancing softness, flavor, structure, and freshness rather than chasing shelf life or perfectly uniform industrial results.

We keep production small enough that each batch gets real attention. The product images on this site are our actual breads, photographed as baked in the kitchen.

How We Bake

Quality here is mostly process discipline, not marketing language.

These are the practical decisions that shape how the bread tastes, feels, and keeps.

Small batches first

We bake in limited quantities so the bread reaches you fresher and with less holding time than mass-produced loaves.

Slower fermentation

Time is part of the recipe. Longer fermentation helps build flavor, aroma, and a more natural structure before the dough is baked.

Baked closer to order

Orders confirmed by 5pm are generally prepared for next-morning delivery, which keeps the bakery aligned around freshness instead of stockholding.

Photographed as baked

What you see on the site is our own bread from the kitchen, not stock photography and not over-styled food imagery.

Ingredients & Standards

Ingredient choices stay simple, but the reasoning still matters.

  • Flour is sourced from established Delhi mills rather than packaged shelf flour designed for long retail storage.
  • Milk, butter, and cream are sourced from local dairies where freshness and quality matter more than extended shelf life.
  • Olives, walnuts, honey, oats, seeds, and enrichments are used where the bread genuinely benefits from them.
  • Ingredients are chosen for flavor, texture, and how the bread actually eats — not simply for label marketing.

Bread Guide

What Makes our Bread Feel Different

Why bread can feel different

Without improvers and softeners, the crumb is more natural and less engineered. Higher whole wheat loaves will also feel denser by design.

Why bread dries faster

Simple-ingredient bread without softeners and preservatives will dry faster if left exposed to air. Covered room-temperature storage or freezing works best.

Why 100% whole wheat is not the default

Very high whole wheat percentages can become much denser. Our blends are designed to keep a better balance of softness, flavor, and structure.

Is white flour always unhealthy?

Not automatically. White flour is more refined than whole wheat, so removing the bran and germ lowers the fiber and reduces some vitamins, minerals, and protein that come from those outer layers. But the inner part of the grain still provides starch, some protein, and useful nutrition. In bread, white flour also gives a softer texture and can feel easier to digest for some people. The better choice depends on the kind of bread you want and how it fits into the rest of your diet, not on a simple "good" or "bad" label.
